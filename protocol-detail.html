Indications & Goals
Population
Adult ICU patients on mechanical ventilation or with RASS ≥ +2
Targets
Pain NRS/CPOT ≤ 3, RASS 0 to −2, CAM-ICU negative
Frequency
At least every 4 hours; hourly when titrating sedation
2
RASS (Richmond Agitation-Sedation Scale)
Sedation & agitation assessment — 10-point scale
+4
Combative
Overtly combative or violent; immediate danger to staff
+3
Very agitated
Pulls on or removes tubes/catheters; aggressive
+2
Agitated
Frequent non-purposeful movement; fights ventilator
+1
Restless
Anxious but movements not aggressive
0
Alert & calm
Spontaneously pays attention — target state
−1
Drowsy
Sustained eye opening to voice ( ≥ 10 sec )
−2
Light sedation
Briefly opens eyes to voice ( < 10 sec )
−3
Moderate sedation
Movement to voice but no eye contact
−4
Deep sedation
No response to voice; movement to physical stimulation
−5
Unarousable
No response to voice or physical stimulation
3
1st-line Sedation Agents
Per SCCM guidelines · continuous IV infusion
Dexmedetomidine
α2-agonist
Loading 1 µg/kg IV over 10 min (optional)
Maintenance 0.2 – 1.4 µg/kg/hr
Preferred for lower delirium risk. Monitor for bradycardia and hypotension.
Propofol
GABA-A
Infusion 5 – 50 µg/kg/min
Titrate by 5 µg/kg/min q 5 min
Monitor for PRIS (lactate, CK, triglycerides) if > 48 h or high-dose.
Midazolam
Benzodiazepine
Infusion 0.02 – 0.1 mg/kg/hr
2nd-line due to delirium risk. Accumulates in hepatic/renal impairment.
Avoid benzodiazepines: associated with prolonged ventilation, longer ICU stay, and higher delirium incidence. Reserve for cases with no alternative and use the lowest effective dose.
