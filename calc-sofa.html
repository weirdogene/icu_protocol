SOFA Score
Sequential Organ Failure Assessment
Quantifies organ dysfunction across six systems. Daily change tracks course; increase ≥ 2 defines sepsis in the setting of infection.
01 Respiration — PaO₂/FiO₂
02 Coagulation — Platelets (×10³/μL)
03 Liver — Bilirubin (mg/dL)
04 Cardiovascular — MAP / Vasopressors

Doses in μg/kg/min. NE = norepinephrine; Epi doses follow NE thresholds.

05 CNS — Glasgow Coma Scale
06 Renal — Creatinine / Urine Output

Cr in mg/dL; UO in mL/day.

07 Interpretation
0–6Mortality < 10%
7–9Mortality 15–20%
10–12Mortality 40–50%
13–14Mortality 50–60%
≥ 15Mortality > 80%

Vincent JL, Moreno R, Takala J, et al. The SOFA (Sepsis-related Organ Failure Assessment) score to describe organ dysfunction/failure. Intensive Care Med. 1996;22(7):707-10.
