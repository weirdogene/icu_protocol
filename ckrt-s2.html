처방Prescription

1.기본 처방

(1)CKRT
•Blood flow rate: 100–200 mL/min
•Dialysate flow rate: 800–2000 mL/hr
•Replacement flow rate: 800–2000 mL/hr (pre:post=8:2)
•Net balance: 0 balance ~ negative 200 mL/hr
•Anticoagulation: none / heparin / nafamostat (Futhan)
(2)추적검사: electrolyte, HCO₃⁻, iCa / P / Mg
•6–8 시간 간격으로 시행하다가 안정적이면 12 시간 간격으로 시행

2.Dose

(1)초기 dose
①목표: CKRT dose는 effluent flow rate에 의해 결정되며, 본원에서 초기 목표 dose는 calculated dose (effective effluent flow rate) 25–30 mL/kg/hr
②처방: 환자의 체중에 따라 아래 처방 dose를 참고
③권고되는 dose 보다 높은 dose가 고려되는 경우
•Severe metabolic derangement (예: sepsis, tumor lysis syndrome, rhabdomyolysis), hyperammonemia, intoxication 등
•다음 두 기준에 해당하는 경우: 한 단계 높은 체중대의 dose로 처방
–Serum potassium > 6.5 mmol/L
–Metabolic acidosis (pH < 7.15 이면서 PaCO₂ < 35 mmHg, 또는 serum bicarbonate < 12 mmol/L)
④고려 사항: ECMO circuit을 CKRT route로 사용하는 경우 BFR 150 mL/min 또는 그 이상을 기본으로 처방

(2)Dose de-escalation
①높은 dose를 불필요하게 지속할 경우 protein malnutrition, vitamin & micronutrient deficiency, antimicrobial drug level의 부적절한 하향을 초래할 수 있으므로, 환자 상태를 면밀히 모니터링 하면서 dose를 적절히 조정
②초기 dose 적용 이후 환자 상태 변화에 따라 dose를 조정하되, 환자 상태가 안정적이고 CKRT 시작 후 3일 이상 경과하였으며 다음 조건을 모두 만족하는 경우, dose를 최소 한 단계 감량 권고
•BUN < 30 mg/dL
•Normokalemia
•HCO₃⁻ ≥ 18 mmol/L

* Dose 800 mL/hr를 유지하고 있던 환자의 경우, dose 600 mL/hr까지 감량 가능

3.Net fluid balance

•초기 처방에서 100 mL/hr 이상의 net negative fluid balance를 적용할 때에는 CKRT 담당 신장내과 전임의와 사전 상의가 필요하며, 100 mL/hr 이상의 negative balance를 24시간 이상 적용할 때에는 CKRT 담당 신장내과 전임의와 재상의가 필요

4.CKRT fluid protocol

(1)Hemosol B0
①본원에서의 기본 CKRT fluid
②Hypokalemia 및 hypophosphatemia 발생 가능
•Hemosol B0만 사용할 경우에 CKRT dose가 높을수록, CKRT 유지 기간이 길수록 발생 위험 증가
•교정 및 예방: dialysate와 replacement fluid에 potassium과 phosphate를 포함시킬 수 있는데, 투약 오류와 오염의 위험을 줄이기 위하여 potassium과 phosphate를 직접 혼합하여 조제하지 않고 상용화된 premixed CKRT solution을 적절히 사용
(2)Multibic 4K, Phoxilium
•Hypokalemia 또는 hypophosphatemia에서 사용
–Dialysate solution 및 Pre-replacement fluid: 아래 표준 수액 프로토콜에 따라 Hemosol B0 대신 Multibic 4K 또는 Phoxilium으로 변경하여 사용
–Post-replacement fluid: 특수한 상황을 제외하고는 추가 조정 없이 Hemosol B0로 유지

1.Standard Prescription

(1)CKRT
•Blood flow rate: 100–200 mL/min
•Dialysate flow rate: 800–2000 mL/hr
•Replacement flow rate: 800–2000 mL/hr (pre:post=8:2)
•Net balance: 0 balance ~ negative 200 mL/hr
•Anticoagulation: none / heparin / nafamostat (Futhan)
(2)Laboratory follow-up
•Electrolytes, HCO₃⁻, iCa / P / Mg
•Every 6–8 hours initially; every 12 hours when stable

2.Dose

(1)Initial dose prescription
①Goal: CKRT dose is determined by the effluent flow rate. At our institution, the initial dose target is a calculated dose (effective effluent flow rate) of 25–30 mL/kg/hr
②Prescription: refer to the dose protocol below according to body weight
③Conditions in which a higher-than-recommended dose should be considered:
•Severe metabolic derangement (e.g., sepsis, tumor lysis syndrome, rhabdomyolysis), hyperammonemia, intoxication, etc.
•When either of the following criteria is met: prescribe according to the dose corresponding to the next higher body-weight category
–Serum potassium > 6.5 mmol/L
–Metabolic acidosis (pH < 7.15 with PaCO₂ < 35 mmHg, or serum bicarbonate < 12 mmol/L)
④Considerations: when an ECMO circuit is used as the CKRT route, prescribe a BFR of 150 mL/min or higher as a baseline

(2)Dose de-escalation
①Unnecessarily prolonged use of a high dose may result in protein malnutrition, vitamin and micronutrient deficiency, and inappropriate reduction of antimicrobial drug levels; therefore, the dose should be adjusted appropriately with close monitoring of the patient’s clinical status
②After the initial dose has been applied, the dose should be adjusted according to changes in the patient’s condition. If the patient is clinically stable, more than 3 days have elapsed since CKRT initiation, and all of the following criteria are met, dose reduction by at least one step is recommended:
•BUN < 30 mg/dL
•Normokalemia
•HCO₃⁻ ≥ 18 mmol/L

* For a patient maintained on a dose of 800 mL/hr, dose reduction down to 600 mL/hr is acceptable

3.Net Fluid Balance

•If an initial prescription includes a net negative fluid balance of ≥ 100 mL/hour, prior consultation with the nephrology fellow responsible for CKRT is required. If a negative balance of ≥100 mL/hour is maintained for more than 24 hours, further discussion with the nephrology fellow responsible for CKRT is required

4.CKRT Fluid Protocol

(1)Hemosol B0
①Standard CKRT fluid used at our institution
②Risk of hypokalemia and hypophosphatemia
•Risk of occurrence increases with higher CKRT dose and longer CKRT duration when Hemosol B0 is used alone
•Correction and prevention: potassium and phosphate may be added to the dialysate and replacement fluid; however, to reduce the risk of medication error and contamination, do not directly mix potassium and phosphate manually—use commercially available premixed CKRT solutions appropriately
(2)Multibic 4K, Phoxilium
•Use in hypokalemia or hypophosphatemia
–Dialysate solution and pre-replacement fluid: replace Hemosol B0 with Multibic 4K or Phoxilium according to the standard fluid protocol below
–Post-replacement fluid: maintain Hemosol B0 without additional adjustment, except in special circumstances
